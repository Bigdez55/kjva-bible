## I. PDT IMPLEMENTATION REVIEW

**Finding: P0 -- BLOCKER**

### What is correct
- Rolling 5-business-day window concept is correct per FINRA Rule 4210
- Blocking the 4th day trade when equity < $25,000 is the right threshold
- Using a dedicated `bot_day_trade_log` table for tracking is proper

### What is missing or wrong

**1. Day Trade Definition Edge Case -- CRITICAL**

A "day trade" under FINRA is opening AND closing the same security on the same business day. The proposed plan must handle these edge cases that are NOT addressed:

- **Pre-existing positions:** If the user manually bought 100 shares of AAPL yesterday, and the bot sells those 100 shares today, then buys 100 shares of AAPL today -- that is NOT a day trade on the sell (it closed a prior-day position), but IS a day trade if you then sell the newly-bought 100 shares today. The bot must track entry date per lot, not just per symbol.
- **Partial fills creating day trades:** If a buy order is partially filled (50 of 100 shares) and later the remaining 50 fill, then a sell of 50 shares occurs the same day -- only 50 shares constitute a day trade. The PDT counter must track at the lot level using FIFO or specific identification.
- **Cross-day holds that become day trades:** An order submitted at 3:59 PM that fills after-hours or the next pre-market does NOT count as a same-day trade. The fill timestamp, not the submission timestamp, determines the day trade date.
- **Options exercises/assignments:** If the bot trades options (as proposed), an option assignment that results in stock delivery, followed by a same-day sale of that stock, IS a day trade.

**2. Alpaca PDT Flag Reliance -- CRITICAL**

The current `AlpacaBroker.get_account_info()` already reads `pattern_day_trader` from Alpaca (line 293 of `alpaca.py`). However:

- Alpaca's PDT flag is a TRAILING indicator -- it flags AFTER the violation, not before the 4th trade. The bot must maintain its OWN rolling count and block proactively.
- Alpaca also enforces PDT on their end (they will reject the order), but relying on broker-side rejection as your compliance gate is a HARD_STOP violation of Protocol 1. The compliance check must happen LOCALLY within 10ms BEFORE the order reaches the broker.

**3. Account Equity Calculation**

The $25,000 threshold is based on account equity at the START of the day (per FINRA), not real-time equity. If the account starts the day at $24,900, the PDT restriction applies for the entire day even if positions appreciate to $26,000 intraday. The implementation must snapshot equity at market open (9:30 AM ET) and use that value for PDT gating throughout the day.

### Required Modifications
```
P0-PDT-1: Track day trades at LOT level (FIFO), not symbol level
P0-PDT-2: Use fill_timestamp (not submit_timestamp) for day trade date
P0-PDT-3: Snapshot account equity at 9:30 AM ET daily for PDT threshold
P0-PDT-4: Local pre-trade PDT check (not reliance on Alpaca rejection)
P1-PDT-5: Handle options assignment -> stock sale day trade chain
```

## II. OPTIONS COMPLIANCE REVIEW

**Finding: P0 -- BLOCKER**

### What is correct
- Greeks tracking (delta, gamma, theta, vega, IV) at 60s intervals is appropriate
- 1-DTE auto-close at market open is prudent risk management
- Buying power checks before submission are mandatory

### What is missing or wrong

**1. Alpaca Options API Limitations -- CRITICAL**

Alpaca's options trading support (launched 2024) has significant constraints that the proposal does not address:

- **Options Approval Tiers:** The existing `get_account_features()` in `alpaca.py` (lines 565-622) already reads `options_approved_level`. The proposal MUST gate order types by tier:
  - **Tier 0:** No options (account not approved)
  - **Tier 1:** Covered calls, cash-secured puts, long calls/puts
  - **Tier 2:** Spreads (verticals), straddles, strangles, iron condors
- The proposal's `submit_multileg_order()` for iron condors requires Tier 2. The bot MUST check the tier BEFORE attempting submission and HARD_STOP if the account lacks Tier 2 approval.

**2. Alpaca Multi-Leg Support**

As of the Alpaca API v2, multi-leg options orders are supported but with constraints:
- Maximum 4 legs per order
- Only equity options (no index options)
- Only vertical spreads, iron condors, straddles/strangles natively
- The proposal's multi-leg types (vertical spreads, iron condors, straddles) are within Alpaca's capabilities, but the bot must validate leg count and structure against Alpaca's accepted patterns BEFORE submission.

**3. Options Suitability -- REGULATORY REQUIREMENT**

Per FINRA Rule 2360 (Options), the broker must have an approved Options Agreement on file. This is an Alpaca account-level setting. The bot must:
- Verify `options_approved_level >= 1` before ANY options order
- For complex strategies (multi-leg), verify `options_approved_level >= 2`
- Log the verification with an audit token per Protocol 5

**4. Exercise/Assignment Risk**

The proposal does NOT address:
- **Early assignment risk** on short options (American-style equity options can be assigned at any time). If the bot sells a covered call and it gets assigned, the bot must handle the stock delivery and update positions accordingly.
- **Pin risk** at expiration: Options near the strike at expiration may or may not be exercised. The bot must have a position reconciliation flow post-expiration.
- **Cash settlement vs. physical delivery:** All equity options are physically settled. The bot must have buying power for potential assignments.

**5. Options Greeks Data Source**

The proposal says "Greeks tracking every 60s" but does not specify the data source. Alpaca provides `greeks` on options positions via their API, but for real-time Greeks during trading decisions, the bot may need to calculate them locally (Black-Scholes or binomial) since API-provided Greeks may lag.

### Required Modifications
```
P0-OPT-1: Gate options orders by Alpaca tier (Tier 1/Tier 2) with HARD_STOP
P0-OPT-2: Verify Options Agreement status before first options trade
P0-OPT-3: Add early assignment handler for short options positions
P1-OPT-4: Validate multi-leg structure against Alpaca's accepted patterns
P1-OPT-5: Add post-expiration position reconciliation flow
P2-OPT-6: Define Greeks data source (API vs. local calculation)
```

## III. ORDER STATE MACHINE REVIEW

**Finding: P1 -- CRITICAL**

### What is correct
The existing `_map_broker_status()` in `alpaca.py` (lines 197-217) already handles these Alpaca statuses:
- `filled`, `partially_filled`, `new`, `accepted`, `pending_new`, `accepted_for_bidding`
- `stopped`, `rejected`, `suspended`, `canceled`, `pending_cancel`, `pending_replace`
- `replaced`, `done_for_day`, `expired`

The proposal's state machine (Pending -> PreTradeCheck -> Queued -> Sent -> PartialFill/Filled/Cancelled/Expired) is a SUPERSET of the current implementation, which is correct.

### What is missing or wrong

**1. `REPLACED` Status Handling**

The current code maps `replaced` to `TradeStatus.PENDING`, which is incorrect. A `replaced` order means Alpaca created a NEW order to replace the old one. The old order should be marked `REPLACED` (a terminal state for the old order), and the new order ID must be tracked. The proposed state machine does not include `REPLACED` as a state.

**2. `done_for_day` Mapped to FILLED**

Currently (line 213), `done_for_day` maps to `TradeStatus.FILLED`. This is WRONG. `done_for_day` means the order is done for the day but may NOT be fully filled -- it could be a partially filled order that will not receive additional fills today. It should map to `PARTIALLY_FILLED` or a new `DONE_FOR_DAY` terminal state, with quantity reconciliation.

**3. `suspended` Mapped to PENDING**

`suspended` (line 208) should NOT map to `PENDING`. A suspended order is being held by the exchange/broker due to a halt or other regulatory action. It should be a distinct state so the kill switch and monitoring can react appropriately.

**4. Missing `PENDING_NEW` -> `ACCEPTED` Transition**

The proposal describes a state machine but does not mention the `PENDING_NEW` -> `ACCEPTED` -> `NEW` lifecycle that Alpaca uses. Orders can be in `PENDING_NEW` for a non-trivial duration during market open rush. The kill switch must be able to cancel orders in `PENDING_NEW` state (which Alpaca supports).

### Required Modifications
```
P1-OSM-1: Add REPLACED as terminal state; track replacement order ID
P1-OSM-2: Fix done_for_day mapping (not FILLED; use PARTIALLY_FILLED or new state)
P2-OSM-3: Add SUSPENDED as distinct state (not PENDING)
P2-OSM-4: Document PENDING_NEW -> ACCEPTED -> NEW lifecycle for kill switch
```

## IV. SLIPPAGE TRACKING REVIEW

**Finding: P2 -- QUALITY**

### What is correct
- Tracking `slippage_bps` on every fill is essential and correct
- The existing `TradeExecutor._update_execution_metrics()` already calculates slippage as `abs(current_price - signal_price) / signal_price`

### Analysis: VWAP as Benchmark

For a personal account with likely small position sizes (<$50K/trade):
- **VWAP benchmark is appropriate for intraday equity trades** -- it provides a fair benchmark for comparing against volume-weighted average for the period.
- **For small orders (<1% of ADV), arrival price (implementation shortfall) is more meaningful** than VWAP. The order is so small relative to volume that the difference between VWAP and arrival price is dominated by timing luck, not market impact.
- **For options, VWAP is NOT meaningful.** Options have wide bid-ask spreads and low volume. The correct benchmark is the **midpoint of the NBBO at time of order submission**.

### Recommended Approach
```
- Equities <$10K: Use arrival price (mid-quote at submission time) as benchmark
- Equities $10K-$50K: Use VWAP for the execution window as benchmark
- Options: Use NBBO midpoint at submission time as benchmark
- All: Track slippage_bps = (fill_price - benchmark_price) / benchmark_price * 10000
```

### Required Modifications
```
P2-SLIP-1: Use arrival price benchmark for small equity orders (<$10K)
P2-SLIP-2: Use NBBO midpoint benchmark for options (not VWAP)
P3-SLIP-3: Store benchmark_type alongside slippage_bps in audit trail
```

## V. FAT-FINGER FILTER REVIEW

**Finding: P1 -- CRITICAL**

### What is correct
- Having a fat-finger filter is essential for autonomous bot trading
- The concept of comparing order size to ADV is correct

### What is missing or wrong

**1. ADV Source Reliability**

The proposal says ">3x ADV" but does not specify the ADV source. Concerns:
- **Low-volume stocks:** ADV can be 0 or near-0 for microcaps. A 3x multiplier on zero is zero -- meaning ANY order would be rejected. The filter needs a minimum ADV floor (e.g., if ADV < 1000 shares, reject the order entirely as illiquid).
- **ADV calculation period:** 20-day ADV is standard. The existing `MarketDataService` likely uses yfinance, which can provide this, but the data may be stale for after-hours calculations.
- **ADV for fractional shares:** Alpaca supports fractional shares. If the user trades 0.5 shares of BRK.A, the ADV comparison is meaningless. The filter should use DOLLAR volume, not share volume.

**2. Options with No ADV Data**

Options contracts often have zero volume on many days, especially for illiquid underlyings or far-OTM strikes. The fat-finger filter must:
- Use **open interest** as the liquidity proxy for options (not ADV)
- Reject orders where the option's open interest < 100 contracts (or configurable threshold)
- For multi-leg orders, apply the filter to the LEAST liquid leg

**3. Price Band Filter**

The proposal says ">5% from last trade" which is reasonable, but:
- "Last trade" may be stale (e.g., previous day's close for pre-market orders). Use **current NBBO midpoint** instead.
- For options, 5% is too tight -- options can move 20%+ intraday on volatile underlyings. The price band for options should be dynamic based on the underlying's ATR or the option's delta.

### Required Modifications
```
P1-FAT-1: Use dollar volume (not share volume) for ADV comparison
P1-FAT-2: Set minimum ADV floor (reject illiquid securities)
P1-FAT-3: Use open interest as liquidity proxy for options fat-finger
P2-FAT-4: Use NBBO midpoint (not last trade) for price band check
P2-FAT-5: Dynamic price band for options based on underlying ATR
```

## VI. KILL SWITCH EDGE CASES

**Finding: P1 -- CRITICAL**

### What is correct
- Mass cancel -> poll for confirmation (10s) is proper
- Position flatten with TWAP over 30min for large positions is prudent
- 2FA restart gate is a strong security control (existing TOTP in codebase)

### What is missing or wrong

**1. PENDING_NEW Orders During Kill Switch**

Orders in `PENDING_NEW` state CAN be cancelled via `DELETE /v2/orders/{order_id}` on Alpaca. However:
- The cancel request may take 1-3 seconds to process
- During this window, the order could transition to `ACCEPTED` -> `FILLED`
- The kill switch must: (1) Send cancel, (2) Poll for confirmation, (3) If order filled during cancel attempt, immediately submit a closing order to flatten

**2. Options Assignments After Flatten**

This is a CRITICAL gap. If the bot holds short options and the kill switch flattens equity positions:
- Short calls: If the underlying runs up after flatten, assignment delivers SHORT stock position (catastrophic)
- Short puts: If the underlying drops, assignment forces stock purchase
- The kill switch MUST close ALL options positions FIRST (buy back short options), THEN flatten equity positions. Order of operations matters.

**3. Multi-Leg Kill Complexity**

For iron condors and other multi-leg positions:
- Each leg is a separate position with Alpaca
- Closing one leg without the others creates naked exposure
- The kill switch must close multi-leg positions as a SINGLE multi-leg closing order to maintain risk parity during the unwind

**4. Market Hours Kill Switch**

If the kill switch fires after market close:
- Equity market orders will queue for next open (potentially at a gap)
- Options cannot trade after hours
- The kill switch should use extended-hours IOC (Immediate-Or-Cancel) limit orders for equities, and queue options closes for next market open with a pre-market alert

### Required Modifications
```
P1-KILL-1: Handle PENDING_NEW -> FILLED race during cancel
P0-KILL-2: Close options positions BEFORE equity flatten (assignment risk)
P1-KILL-3: Close multi-leg positions atomically (not leg-by-leg)
P2-KILL-4: Extended-hours IOC orders for after-hours kill switch
```

## VII. AUTO-FLATTEN TIMING

**Finding: P1 -- CRITICAL**

### Analysis: 3:55 PM ET

The proposal auto-flattens day trades at 3:55 PM ET, which provides 5 minutes before close. Assessment:

**For liquid large-caps (AAPL, MSFT, etc.):** 5 minutes is sufficient. Market depth is deep enough that a market order will fill in seconds.

**For mid-caps and options:** 5 minutes is INSUFFICIENT.
- Options market makers widen spreads significantly in the last 5 minutes
- Many options contracts have reduced liquidity after 3:50 PM
- Some exchanges stop accepting options orders at 4:00 PM sharp, with processing delays reducing the effective window

**For illiquid positions:** 5 minutes is dangerous.
- The existing `RiskManagementService` default `max_trade_value` is $50K (line 123 of `risk_management.py`)
- A $50K position in a stock with 50K ADV share volume would represent a full day's volume
- The current `CircuitBreaker` has a LIQUIDITY type but no implementation for auto-flatten timing adjustment

### Recommended Tiered Approach
```
- Liquid equities (ADV > $10M): Auto-flatten at 3:55 PM ET
- Mid-cap equities ($1M < ADV < $10M): Auto-flatten at 3:45 PM ET
- Illiquid equities (ADV < $1M): Auto-flatten at 3:30 PM ET
- Options (all): Auto-flatten at 3:45 PM ET
- Use LIMIT orders (not MARKET) with aggressive pricing for the flatten
```

### Required Modifications
```
P1-FLAT-1: Tiered auto-flatten timing based on liquidity profile
P1-FLAT-2: Use limit orders (not market) for flatten with aggressive pricing
P2-FLAT-3: Escalation alert if flatten order not filled by 3:58 PM ET
```

## VIII. EXECUTION ALGO APPROPRIATENESS

**Finding: P2 -- QUALITY (Overengineered, but not harmful)**

### Honest Assessment

For a personal account with likely position sizes <$50K per trade:

**TWAP:** Marginally useful for positions >$20K in mid-cap stocks. For typical bot trades of $1K-$10K in large-caps, TWAP adds latency without reducing market impact (because there IS no meaningful market impact).

**VWAP:** Same assessment as TWAP. Only meaningful if order size is >1% of expected volume for the execution window.

**Implementation Shortfall (Almgren-Chriss):** Overkill. This algorithm is designed for institutional orders of $1M+ that cause measurable price impact. For a personal account, the optimization surface is essentially flat.

**POV (5% participation rate):** Irrelevant for orders that represent <0.01% of daily volume.

**RL Execution (SAC agent):** This is the most concerning. An RL agent for order sizing on a personal account:
- Has no meaningful training data (personal account generates too few trades)
- Introduces model risk that exceeds the execution alpha it could capture
- The fallback chain (RL -> VWAP -> TWAP) suggests awareness of this, which is good

### What I Actually Recommend

For a personal trading account:

```
1. DEFAULT: Smart limit orders (limit price = NBBO midpoint + 1-2 ticks for buys)
2. FOR POSITIONS >$20K: TWAP over 5-10 minutes with limit slices
3. KILL SWITCH FLATTEN: Market orders (speed > price)
4. OPTIONS: Limit orders at mid-spread or better. NEVER market orders on options.
5. DEFER: VWAP, IS, POV, and RL until the account is managing >$500K
```

The existing `TradeExecutor._create_order()` (line 127) already defaults to `OrderType.MARKET`. This should be changed to `OrderType.LIMIT` with smart pricing for all non-emergency orders.

### Required Modifications
```
P2-EXEC-1: Default to limit orders (not market) for non-emergency trades
P2-EXEC-2: Implement smart limit pricing (NBBO midpoint + N ticks)
P3-EXEC-3: Defer VWAP/IS/POV/RL until account exceeds $500K AUM
P0-EXEC-4: NEVER use market orders for options (HARD_STOP constraint)
```

## IX. AUDIT TRAIL FOR TAX REPORTING

**Finding: P1 -- CRITICAL**

### What exists
The current `TradeDecisionLog` (file: `backend/app/models/trade_decision_log.py`) captures:
- Decision inputs: market_context, model_prompt, model_raw_response
- Decision output: action, confidence, reasoning, risk_factors
- Execution link: trade_id, price_at_decision
- Outcome tracking: price_at_1h, price_at_4h, price_at_1d

The `Trade` model (file: `backend/app/models/trade.py`) captures:
- Symbol, side, quantity, price, filled_price, filled_quantity
- Timestamps: created_at, updated_at, executed_at, filled_at
- is_paper_trade flag

### What is missing for tax compliance

**1. Wash Sale Rule Tracking -- CRITICAL**

The proposal mentions wash-sale rule in the compliance kill conditions, but there is NO implementation. For a bot that may trade the same symbol multiple times:
- IRS wash sale rule (Section 1091): A loss is disallowed if you buy "substantially identical" securities within 30 days before or after the sale
- The bot MUST maintain a 61-day rolling window per symbol
- Disallowed losses must be added to the cost basis of the replacement shares
- This affects tax lot tracking and 1099-B reporting

**2. Cost Basis Method**

The existing `Holding` model uses `average_cost`. For tax purposes:
- Alpaca defaults to FIFO for equities
- The bot must know which cost basis method is being used (FIFO, LIFO, specific identification, average cost) and track it consistently
- For options, each contract is its own lot

**3. Section 1256 Contracts**

If the bot trades options:
- Equity options are NOT Section 1256 contracts (they use normal capital gains rules)
- But if the bot ever trades broad-based index options (SPX, NDX), those ARE Section 1256 contracts with 60/40 long-term/short-term treatment
- The audit trail must tag each options trade with its tax classification

**4. 1099-B Data Sufficiency**

For year-end tax filing, the audit trail must provide:
- Date acquired, date sold, proceeds, cost basis, gain/loss
- Holding period (short-term vs. long-term -- >1 year)
- Wash sale adjustment amount
- Whether the trade was a "covered" security (reported to IRS by broker)

### Required Modifications
```
P0-TAX-1: Implement wash sale detection (61-day rolling window per symbol)
P0-TAX-2: Add wash sale disallowed loss to replacement lot cost basis
P1-TAX-3: Track cost basis method (FIFO/specific ID) consistently
P1-TAX-4: Add holding_period_type (short_term/long_term) to Trade model
P2-TAX-5: Tag options trades with tax classification (regular vs. 1256)
```

## X. REGULATORY RISK -- ALPACA TOS & AUTONOMOUS BOT

**Finding: P0 -- BLOCKER**

### Alpaca Terms of Service

Alpaca explicitly PERMITS algorithmic and automated trading. Their API is designed for this purpose. This is NOT a TOS violation.

However, there are specific requirements:

**1. Alpaca Algo Trading Agreement**

Alpaca requires that algorithmic traders:
- Accept the Alpaca Algo Trading Addendum (separate from the standard Customer Agreement)
- Acknowledge that they are responsible for all orders generated by their algorithm
- Maintain "kill switch" functionality (which the proposal includes -- good)

Verify that this addendum has been accepted for the account before enabling live mode.

**2. Options-Specific Requirements**

For autonomous options trading:
- Alpaca requires a signed Options Agreement
- The account must pass Alpaca's suitability assessment
- Tier 2 approval requires meeting Alpaca's financial criteria
- The `get_account_features()` in `alpaca.py` already checks these -- ensure the bot gates on them

**3. SEC Regulation SHO (Short Selling)**

If the bot ever generates a short sale signal:
- Regulation SHO requires a "locate" before short selling
- Alpaca handles locates for their customers, but the bot should verify the security is "easy to borrow" before submitting a short sale
- The proposal does not mention short selling -- if it is not planned, add a HARD_STOP that prevents the bot from submitting any short sale orders

**4. Market Manipulation Risk**

An autonomous bot that trades the same symbols repeatedly could theoretically create patterns that resemble:
- **Spoofing:** Placing and canceling orders to manipulate price (unlikely with the current market-order-default, but possible with the proposed limit order strategies)
- **Layering:** Creating a false impression of supply/demand
- The cancel rate should be monitored. If the bot cancels >50% of its orders, this warrants investigation and logging as a SAR-adjacent event.

**5. FINRA Rule 3110 (Supervision)**

For a personal account, you ARE the supervisory authority. However:
- The audit trail must demonstrate that you reviewed the bot's activity periodically (daily log review)
- The kill switch + 2FA restart gate satisfies the "ability to intervene" requirement
- Consider a daily summary email/notification of all trades executed

### Required Modifications
```
P0-REG-1: Verify Alpaca Algo Trading Addendum acceptance before live mode
P0-REG-2: HARD_STOP on short sales unless explicitly enabled and locate verified
P1-REG-3: Monitor cancel rate; flag if >50% of orders are cancelled
P1-REG-4: Daily activity summary notification for supervisory compliance
P2-REG-5: Document periodic review schedule (FINRA 3110 compliance)
```
